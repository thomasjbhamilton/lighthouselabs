PEPPERED LAMB STEAK

Hide Images

" If you are eating in the backyard, I suggest you serve the lamb still warm. Otherwise, for a picnic, take the cooked steaks with you; slice them thickly and scatter on top of the salad before applying the dressing. "

Serves 4 ** click on ingredients you already own!

  • 2 tbsp (30ml) black peppercorns
  • 4 lamb leg steaks, each 9oz (250g), About 3/4 inch (2cm)thick
  • 2 tbsp (30ml) olive oil

Crush the peppercorns ** click to complete steps!

Lightly, using the mortar and pestle, then tip into a strainer and shake to get rid of the fine dust. Tip the crushed peppercorns onto a plate and press both sides of the steaks onto the peppercorns to coat.

Heat the olive oil

In a large skillet. Add the lamb steaks and pan-fry over high heat for 2 to 3 minutes on each side. Remove from the skillet and let rest for 5 minutes.

Slice the peppered lamb

thickly and serve alongside (or on top of) the salad. Serve warm or cold, with crusty bread.

ADD A TASTY SALAD

" Now that we have the lamb sorted out, we need to create the salad! "

Add the beans

to a pan of boiling salted water and cok for 4 minutes or until just tender. Drain, refresh in cold water, then pat dry with paper towels.

Put the onion

in a colander, sprinkle with salt, and leave for 5 minutes. Holding the colander over the sink, pour on a kettleful of boiling water to remove some of the onion acidity. Drain well and pat dry.

Toss the beans,

onion and romano together in a bowl. For the dressing, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, and salt & pepper to taste (or shake to emulsify in a screw topped jar).

Drizzle the dressing

over the salad to serve. Enjoy immensly!

Serves 4 ** click on ingredients you already own!

  • 14oz (400g) green beans, trimmed
  • Sea salt & black pepper
  • 1 red onion, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 20oz (50g) romano, freshly grated
  • 2-3 tbsp (30-40ml) extra virgin olive oil
  • juice of 1/2 lemon
